#Que 1 :

(sec^2 θ − 1)(cosec^2 θ − 1) = 1

Ans

Solution:
Using identities,
(sec^2 θ − tan^2 θ) = 1 and (cosec^2 θ − cot^2 θ) = 1
We have,
L.H.S. = (sec^2 θ – 1)(cosec^2θ – 1)
= tan^2θ × cot^2θ
= (tan θ × cot θ)^2
= (tan θ × 1/tan θ)^2
= 1^2
= 1
= R.H.S.
– Hence, proved.

#Que 2 :

tan θ + 1/ tan θ = sec θ cosec θ

Ans

Solution:
We have,
L.H.S. = tan θ + 1/ tan θ
= (tan^2 θ + 1)/ tan θ
= sec^2 θ / tan θ [∵ sec^2 θ − tan^2 θ = 1]
= cos θ/ (sin θ x cos^2 θ)
= 1/ cos θ x 1/ sin θ
= sec θ x cosec θ
= sec θ cosec θ
= R.H.S.
– Hence, proved

#Que 3 :

tan^2 θ cos^2 θ = 1 − cos^2 θ

Ans

Solution:
We know that,
sin^2 θ + cos^2 θ = 1
Taking,
L.H.S. = tan^2 θ cos^2 θ
= (tan θ × cos θ)^2
= (sin θ)^2
= sin^2 θ
= 1 – cos^2 θ
= R.H.S.
– Hence, proved.
